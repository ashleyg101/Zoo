Loggerhead Sea Turtle

Caretta caretta

Loggerhead Sea Turtles inhabit coastal bays, estuaries, and shallow waters. They nest at intervals of 2-4 years. Newborn eggs and hatchlings are vulnerable to predation by other mammals on land and in the water. This species is endangered by nesting habitat loss, predation of nests, harvest of turtles and eggs, fishing bycatch, coastal development, ocean pollution, and climate change.

  • color: reddish-brown/yellowish-brown
  • lifespan: 70-80 years
  • diet: carnivorous
  • predators: sharks, humans
