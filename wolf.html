Red Wolf

Canis rufus

As the most endangered wolf species, the Red Wolf can be found in forests, swamps, or coastal prairies
only within North Carolina. They are social mammals that form packs of five to eight wolves. Less than half of
the pups that are born in the wild grow to adulthood as a result of disease, malnutrition, and predation.
The existence of Red Wolves is threatened by hunting, habitat loss and
fragmentation, hybridization, and competition with coyotes.

  • color: brown/black, red tint
  • lifespan: 6-7 years
  • diet: carnivorous
  • communication: body language, scent marking, vocalizations
